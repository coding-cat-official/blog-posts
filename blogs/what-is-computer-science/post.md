### What really is Computer Science? 

Most people think computer science is just about writing code. But it is the study of what computers can do and the answer to that question turns out to be almost everything. 

It is also worth noting how young this field actually is. Computer science did not exist as a discipline until the mid-20th century, yet it has grown faster than any other field in history. 

Your phone has more computing power than what existed in the entire world in the mid-1960s. The Apollo moon landing could have been run on a couple of Nintendo consoles. Computers started as arithmetic machines and grew into tools that run the internet, create graphics and simulate the universe. All of it comes down to flipping zeros and ones. 

So what does computer science actually cover? It can be broken down into three parts: theory, engineering and applications. 

 

### The foundation of Computer Science 

The theoretical foundation of computer science begins with Alan Turing. 

Turing created the idea of a Turing machine, which is a simple model of how a computer works. It has a tape to store information, a head to read and write on that tape and a list of instructions to follow. Every computer today is built on that same idea. 

<img class="cs" alt="Turing machine" src="/blog-images/turing-machine.jpg">

Some problems are impossible for any computer to solve. It doesn’t matter how powerful they are. Others are possible but would take longer to figure out. 

Algorithms are at heart of theory. An algorithm is a set of steps to solve a problem like a recipe on how to build a program. Different algorithms can reach the same answer, but some are more efficient than others. Finding the best algorithm for a given problem is a big part of what computer scientists do. 

 

### From hardware to software 

You also need a machine to run the theory on, and that is what computer engineering is about. 

Every computer has a CPU, which is like a brain that handles all the work. When multiple programs run at once, the CPU switches between jobs quickly, so nothing gets stuck.  

On top of the hardware, there’s the software using programming languages. That’s how we give computer instructions. Languages like Python are easy for humans to write, but the computer needs them translated into something it understands, called machine code. That job belongs to the compiler. 

The operating system manages hardware, memory and programs to ensure that everything operates together. 

It took decades of dedicated engineering to build the computers we use today. There is still room for the next generation of students to push it even further. If hardware or engineering interests you, you could be part of what comes next. 

 

### Branches of CS 

Theory and engineering merge together to solve actual problems accross many specialized fields. Here is what computer science covers: 

Branch 

Key questions 

Artificial Intelligence 

How can we get algorithms to learn without being explicitly programmed?  

How do machines make decisions? 

 

Machine Learning & Computer Vision 

How can computers recognize images, patterns and language the way humans do? 

 

Natural Language Processing 

How can computers understand and communicate using human language? 

 

Human-Computer Interaction 

How can we design computers that make it easy for humans to interact with? How does the interface of software change how people use it? 

 

Robotics 

How do we give computers a physical form that can interact with the world? 

 

Cybersecurity 

How do we find and fix weaknesses in computer systems before they are exploited? 

 

Big Data 

How do we store, manage and find value in big amounts of information? 

 

Computational Science 

How can computers help us answer complex scientific questions? 

 

Augmented & Virtual Reality 

How can computers enhance or replace what we see and experience around us? 

 

Optimisation 

How do we find the best solution out of millions of possibilities? 

 

 

Computer science is too broad for anyone to master it all and no one is expecting you to. The goal is to find the questions that interests you and dive into it. 

 

### Endless possibilities 

Computer science is one of the fastest moving fields. New branches keep emerging and old ones keep growing. It shapes communication, medicine, entertainment and finance on how societies function. 

Understanding the full map of computer science helps anyone make sense of the world we live in and where it might be heading next.
