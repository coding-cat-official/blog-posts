The term “Computer Science” is a very broad one, covering a large rage of subdomains all centered around using technology to communicate. It is therefore hard to say with certainty whether someone would enjoy computer science or not. However, there are commonalities between different domains that can help you understand whether you’d have a good time in this field.

### You could enjoy computer science if you… 

#### Enjoy creating solutions 

There is never a single solution for a given problem in computer science. There are many, and they all have their tradeoffs. It is up to you to find the best way to accomplish a certain task. If you like looking at a problem to <span class="bold">figure out the best possible solution</span> for it, computer science is for you. 

#### Like spending time analyzing a problem 

Also known as <span class="bold">debugging</span>! In computer science, bugs do appear. If you think you’d enjoy hunting down bugs through your code, slowly eliminating potential causes until you find that pesky little thing, you should definitely give computer science a shot. 

#### Want to become more perseverant 

I, the one writing this, am a student just like you. I used to be someone with a defeatist mentality. However, comp sci has trained my mental endurance, and I no longer despair when looking at bugs. No matter how much of an expert we may become, we will always encounter bugs or other problems. It is important not to get discouraged at the slightest issue. If you want to develop your <span class="bold">patience and perseverance</span>, computer science is a good fit. 

#### Are creative 

Computer science can be an art. There is beauty in finding the most elegant solution to a certain problem and feeling everything click into place. The most innovative creations in computer science were made by people <span class="bold">thinking outside the box</span>, like Lynn Conway, pioneer of the microchip. If you like creative pursuits, computer science can be a good complement to that. Computation Arts are also a domain worth looking into for more artistic-leaning students. 

#### Like continuous learning 

Your role in the industry will change as the industry itself changes. If you have a curious mind and want to <span class="bold">keep on learning</span> throughout your career, this is the place for you. There is always more to know in computer science. 

### The Ever-Changing Landscape of CS 

Computer science is an ever-evolving field where being out of the loop for a few months may mean falling behind. There are always new technologies, languages, workflows, and schools of thinking emerging.  

Ever since the inception of human-readable programming languages, there have been some that trend for a while, only to be replaced by a newer language, which itself eventually gets replaced. Assembly gave us C, which got replaced by Java, and Java itself is now becoming obsolete in favour of Python. 

Right now, computer science is seeing a huge shift in terms of booming AI development. 

This goes to show that computer science isn’t the kind of place where you can have the same career for 40 years. There is an <span class="bold">immense variety of domains</span> for you to explore. 

### The Human Behind the Machine 

There is a bit of a stereotype for the “average comp sci major”, which paints computer science students as anti-social, basement-dwelling creatures. But computer science actually involves lots of communication, because <span class="bold">on the other side of the computer there is always another human</span>. 

First of all, there is your fellow developer. Any large project must have a proportionally large team working on it, and those teams need to be able to communicate. Developers spend lots of time writing documentation for code, or manuals for hardware, all that to ensure they’re understood by other programmers.
